Steve Jobs

Remembered as a visionary entrepreneur who reshaped technology, design, and media.

Here's a time line of Steve Jobs's life:

  • Full Name: Steven Paul Jobs
  • Born: February 24, 1955, in San Francisco, California, USA
  • Adopted: By Paul and Clara Jobs
  • Attended Reed College but dropped out after six months
  • Took calligraphy classes, which later influenced Apple’s design philosophy
  • 1976: Co-founded Apple with Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne in his parents’ garage
  • 1984: Launched the Macintosh, the first mass-market computer with a graphical user interface
  • 1985: Forced out of Apple and founded NeXT, a company focused on advanced computer technology
  • 1986: Acquired Pixar from Lucasfilm, which became a leader in animated films
  • 1997: Returned to Apple when the company acquired NeXT and became CEO again
  • 2001: Introduced the iPod, revolutionizing the music industry
  • 2007: Launched the iPhone, transforming mobile technology
  • 2010: Released the iPad, popularizing tablets
  • Married Laurene Powell in 1991, had three children
  • Had a daughter, Lisa Brennan-Jobs, from a previous relationship
  • Diagnosed with pancreatic cancer in 2003
  • Resigned as Apple’s CEO in August 2011
  • Passed away: October 5, 2011
